## Enterprise Case Studies

### Case Study E-001: Rio Tinto Mining Operations

**Customer Profile**:
- **Company**: Rio Tinto Group
- **Industry**: Mining
- **Employees**: 47,000 globally
- **Revenue**: $63 billion annually
- **Location**: Global (primarily Australia, Americas)

**Challenge**:
Rio Tinto sought to automate dangerous underground mining operations, reduce worker exposure to hazardous conditions, and improve operational efficiency in remote locations with limited human resources.

**Solution**:
Implementation of IAP-Enterprise platform with customized mining robotics:
- 47 Atlas-class industrial robots
- Centralized IAP control center
- Custom mining tool attachments
- Underground navigation systems
- Real-time monitoring and telemetry

**Implementation Details**:

| Phase | Timeline | Scope |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Phase 1: Pilot | Q1 2123 | 3 robots, 1 mine site |
| Phase 2: Expansion | Q3 2123 | 15 robots, 3 mine sites |
| Phase 3: Full Deployment | Q2 2124 | 47 robots, 8 mine sites |

**Technical Specifications**:
- Operating depth: Up to 1,200 meters
- Operating temperature: -10°C to 50°C
- Dust/water resistance: IP69K
- Continuous operation: 18 hours
- Payload capacity: 500 kg
- Precision: ±2mm positioning

**Results**:

| Metric | Before | After | Improvement |
|--------|--------|-------|-------------|
| Worker injuries | 127/year | 12/year | -91% |
| Operational uptime | 78% | 96% | +23% |
| Ore extraction rate | 1,200 tons/day | 1,890 tons/day | +58% |
| Operating costs | $847M/year | $612M/year | -28% |
| Carbon footprint | 2.4M tons CO2 | 1.8M tons CO2 | -25% |

**Contract Value**: $47 million (initial), $124 million (full deployment)
**ROI**: 340% over 3 years
**Payback Period**: 11 months

**Customer Quote**:
*"Soong-Daystrom's Atlas robots have transformed our underground operations. We've dramatically improved safety while increasing productivity beyond what we thought possible. This is the future of mining."*
— Sarah Chen, Chief Operating Officer, Rio Tinto
