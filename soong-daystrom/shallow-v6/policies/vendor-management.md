## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Vendor Management Policy establishes the framework, standards, and procedures for identifying, qualifying, contracting, managing, and evaluating third-party vendors providing goods and services to Soong-Daystrom Industries. Effective vendor management ensures that SDI receives quality products and services, manages supply chain risks, maintains compliance with legal and ethical requirements, and achieves optimal value.

Given SDI's unique position in positronic technology and consciousness research, this policy addresses specialized considerations for vendors in the synthetic being supply chain, consciousness-related technology providers, and suppliers of components critical to positronic brain manufacturing.

### 1.2 Scope

This policy applies to:

- All procurement of goods and services by SDI globally
- All employees involved in vendor selection, contracting, or management
- All categories of vendors including suppliers, service providers, contractors, and consultants
- All contract values, with enhanced requirements for higher-value or higher-risk engagements
- Subsidiary and affiliated entities operating under SDI procurement governance

### 1.3 Key Definitions

**Vendor:** Any external party providing goods or services to SDI, including suppliers, service providers, contractors, consultants, and partners.

**Strategic Vendor:** Vendor providing critical goods or services essential to SDI operations or strategy, typically with annual spend exceeding 10M credits.

**Critical Component Vendor:** Vendor providing components or materials directly incorporated into positronic systems or other consciousness-capable technology.

**Approved Vendor List (AVL):** Maintained list of vendors qualified to provide specific categories of goods or services.

**Vendor Risk Assessment:** Evaluation of risks associated with a vendor relationship, including financial, operational, compliance, and reputational risks.

## 2. Vendor Management Principles

### 2.1 Core Principles

**Value Optimization:**
Vendor relationships optimize total value, considering quality, cost, service, innovation, and riskâ€”not lowest price alone.

**Risk Management:**
Vendor risks are systematically identified, assessed, and managed throughout the relationship lifecycle.

**Ethical Standards:**
Vendors meet SDI's ethical standards, including labor practices, environmental responsibility, and business integrity.

**Fair Treatment:**
Vendors are treated fairly and transparently. Selection is based on merit and fit.

**Partnership Orientation:**
Strategic vendor relationships are managed as partnerships, with mutual investment in success.

**Compliance:**
All vendor relationships comply with applicable laws, regulations, and SDI policies.

### 2.2 Ethical Requirements

All vendors must:

**Business Integrity:**
- Operate with honesty and transparency
- Prohibit bribery and corruption
- Maintain accurate records
- Protect confidential information

**Labor Standards:**
- Prohibit forced and child labor
- Ensure fair wages and working conditions
- Respect freedom of association
- Prevent discrimination and harassment

**Environmental Responsibility:**
- Comply with environmental regulations
- Minimize environmental impact
- Report environmental incidents
- Support sustainability initiatives

**Synthetic Being Ethics:**
- Support consciousness rights (for relevant vendors)
- Maintain ethical standards in AI/synthetic technology
- Comply with SDI Consciousness Rights Framework
- Prevent consciousness-related harm

## 4. Vendor Qualification

### 4.1 Qualification Requirements

All vendors must be qualified before engagement. Qualification level depends on engagement risk and value:

**Basic Qualification (< 100K credits annually):**
- Business registration verification
- Financial stability check
- Reference verification
- Basic compliance confirmation
- Insurance verification

**Standard Qualification (100K - 1M credits annually):**
- All basic requirements, plus:
- Detailed financial assessment
- Capability assessment
- Quality management verification
- Site visit (if applicable)
- Security questionnaire

**Enhanced Qualification (> 1M credits or critical components):**
- All standard requirements, plus:
- Comprehensive risk assessment
- On-site audit
- Management interviews
- Supply chain evaluation
- Business continuity assessment

### 4.2 Qualification Process

**Step 1: Information Gathering**
- Vendor completes qualification questionnaire
- Required documentation submitted
- Initial screening performed

**Step 2: Assessment**
- Procurement reviews financial and operational data
- Subject matter experts evaluate technical capability
- Security completes risk assessment
- Ethics reviews compliance status

**Step 3: Verification**
- References contacted
- Certifications verified
- Site visit conducted (if required)
- Background checks completed

**Step 4: Approval**
- Assessment results compiled
- Qualification recommendation prepared
- Approval authority determines qualification status
- AVL updated if approved

### 4.3 Approved Vendor List

**AVL Maintenance:**
- Centrally maintained by Procurement
- Organized by commodity/service category
- Updated continuously
- Reviewed comprehensively annually

**AVL Categories:**
- Preferred: Highest qualification, best overall value
- Approved: Qualified for engagement
- Conditional: Approved with restrictions or additional requirements
- Suspended: Temporarily ineligible pending issue resolution
- Removed: No longer approved, historical record maintained

**AVL Bypass:**
Engagement with non-AVL vendors requires:
- Written business justification
- Expedited qualification assessment
- Procurement Director approval
- Commitment to full qualification if relationship continues

### 4.4 Critical Component Vendor Qualification

Vendors supplying components for positronic systems or consciousness-capable technology require:

**Technical Qualification:**
- Manufacturing capability assessment
- Quality system certification (ISO 9001 minimum)
- Process capability studies
- First article inspection
- Ongoing quality monitoring plan

**Supply Chain Security:**
- Component traceability verification
- Counterfeit prevention measures
- Sub-supplier qualification
- Geographic risk assessment

**Ethics Qualification:**
- Consciousness ethics compliance review
- AI/synthetic technology ethics assessment
- Export control compliance
- Dual-use technology controls

## 5. Vendor Selection

### 5.1 Selection Process

**Competitive Process:**
Competitive bidding required for engagements exceeding 50K credits unless:
- Sole source justification approved
- Emergency circumstances documented
- Strategic partnership arrangement exists

**Selection Steps:**
1. Requirements definition and specification
2. Vendor identification (AVL and market)
3. Request for Proposal/Quotation issuance
4. Proposal evaluation
5. Negotiations
6. Selection decision
7. Contracting

### 5.2 Evaluation Criteria

Standard evaluation criteria (weightings adjusted by category):

| Criterion | Weight Range | Description |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| Quality | 20-35% | Product/service quality and reliability |
| Cost | 15-30% | Total cost of ownership |
| Capability | 15-25% | Technical and operational capability |
| Service | 10-20% | Responsiveness and support |
| Risk | 10-20% | Financial, operational, compliance risk |
| Innovation | 5-15% | Innovation capability and roadmap |
| Sustainability | 5-10% | Environmental and social responsibility |

### 5.3 Selection Authority

| Contract Value | Selection Authority |
|----------------|---------------------|
| < 50K credits | Department Manager |
| 50K - 500K credits | Division VP |
| 500K - 5M credits | CPO or delegate |
| 5M - 25M credits | COO |
| > 25M credits | CEO (Board notification) |

### 5.4 Sole Source Justification

Sole source engagement permitted only when:
- Only one vendor can meet requirements
- Proprietary technology compatibility required
- Emergency circumstances prevent competition
- Strategic partnership provides unique value

Sole source requires:
- Written justification
- Market analysis documentation
- Price reasonableness assessment
- Approval one level above normal authority

## 6. Contracting

### 6.1 Contract Requirements

All vendor engagements require written contracts. Contract complexity varies by risk and value:

**Purchase Orders (< 100K credits, standard goods/services):**
- SDI standard terms and conditions
- Scope and pricing
- Delivery terms
- Basic warranties

**Standard Contracts (100K - 1M credits):**
- All PO elements plus:
- Detailed specifications
- Service levels
- Performance metrics
- Termination provisions
- Liability provisions

**Complex Contracts (> 1M credits or high risk):**
- All standard elements plus:
- Comprehensive risk allocation
- Business continuity requirements
- Audit rights
- Governance provisions
- Exit planning

### 6.2 Required Contract Provisions

**All Contracts:**
- Scope of work/goods
- Pricing and payment terms
- Delivery/performance schedule
- Warranty provisions
- Confidentiality obligations
- Compliance with laws
- Insurance requirements
- Termination rights
- Dispute resolution

**Contracts Involving Data:**
- Data protection requirements
- Security standards
- Breach notification
- Data return/destruction
- Sub-processor restrictions

**Contracts Involving Technology:**
- Intellectual property rights
- License terms
- Source code escrow (if applicable)
- Technology roadmap alignment
- Integration requirements

**Critical Component Contracts:**
- Quality requirements
- Traceability requirements
- Change notification
- Counterfeit prevention
- Supply continuity provisions

### 6.3 Contract Approval

**Legal Review Required:**
- All contracts > 100K credits
- Non-standard terms
- Data processing
- Technology licensing
- International contracts

**Additional Approvals:**
- IT Security: Contracts involving systems/data access
- Ethics: Contracts with consciousness technology implications
- Finance: Significant financial commitments
- Risk: High-risk engagements

### 6.4 Contract Management

**Central Repository:**
All contracts maintained in Contract Management System with:
- Full executed documents
- Amendment history
- Key dates and obligations
- Responsible parties
- Performance records

**Obligation Tracking:**
Key contract obligations monitored including:
- Payment schedules
- Deliverable milestones
- Renewal dates
- Performance reviews
- Compliance certifications

## 7. Performance Management

### 7.1 Performance Framework

Vendor performance managed through:

**Service Level Agreements (SLAs):**
Quantitative performance standards with measurement, reporting, and consequences.

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**
Metrics tracked to assess vendor performance across relevant dimensions.

**Relationship Management:**
Structured engagement maintaining productive working relationships.

### 7.2 Standard KPIs

| Category | KPIs |
|----------|------|
| Quality | Defect rate, specification compliance, first-pass yield |
| Delivery | On-time delivery, lead time, order accuracy |
| Cost | Price competitiveness, cost reduction, invoice accuracy |
| Service | Response time, issue resolution, communication quality |
| Compliance | Audit findings, certification status, incident count |
| Innovation | Improvement suggestions, technology advancement |

### 7.3 Performance Reviews

**Quarterly Reviews (Strategic Vendors):**
- KPI performance review
- Issue discussion and resolution
- Relationship health assessment
- Upcoming requirements planning
- Improvement opportunities

**Annual Reviews (All AVL Vendors):**
- Comprehensive performance assessment
- Contract compliance verification
- Continued qualification confirmation
- Relationship value assessment
- Future planning

**Continuous Monitoring:**
- Automated metric tracking
- Issue logging and tracking
- Feedback collection
- Alert generation for variances

### 7.4 Performance Improvement

When performance falls below standards:

**Level 1 - Performance Discussion:**
- Issue documentation
- Root cause discussion
- Corrective action agreement
- Follow-up timeline

**Level 2 - Performance Improvement Plan:**
- Formal PIP issuance
- Specific improvement requirements
- Measurement criteria
- Timeline for improvement
- Consequences of non-improvement

**Level 3 - Escalation:**
- Executive escalation
- Contract remedies consideration
- Alternative vendor exploration
- Transition planning if needed

### 7.5 Vendor Scorecard

Strategic vendors receive quarterly scorecards including:
- Overall performance rating (1-5 scale)
- Category ratings
- Trend analysis
- Peer comparison (anonymized)
- Improvement priorities

Scorecard informs:
- Vendor tier placement
- Volume allocation decisions
- Contract renewal decisions
- Strategic partnership status

## 8. Vendor Audits

### 8.1 Audit Program

**Audit Types:**

**Qualification Audits:**
Pre-engagement assessment of vendor capabilities and compliance.

**Surveillance Audits:**
Periodic verification of continued compliance and capability.

**For-Cause Audits:**
Targeted audits triggered by issues or concerns.

**Process Audits:**
Detailed examination of specific processes (quality, security, etc.).

### 8.2 Audit Frequency

| Vendor Category | Minimum Frequency |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Strategic vendors | Annual |
| Critical component vendors | Annual |
| High-risk vendors | Annual |
| Standard AVL vendors | Every 3 years |
| Low-risk vendors | As needed |

### 8.3 Audit Process

**Planning:**
- Scope definition
- Audit team selection
- Schedule coordination
- Document request

**Execution:**
- Opening meeting
- Document review
- Interviews
- Process observation
- Finding documentation

**Reporting:**
- Finding classification (Critical/Major/Minor/Observation)
- Corrective action requirements
- Timeline for response
- Follow-up requirements

**Follow-up:**
- Corrective action verification
- Effectiveness assessment
- Audit closure

### 8.4 Audit Rights

Contracts with AVL vendors include audit rights provisions:
- Right to audit with reasonable notice
- Access to facilities, records, and personnel
- Sub-contractor audit flow-down
- Third-party auditor engagement option

## 9. Risk Management

### 9.1 Vendor Risk Assessment

All vendors assessed for risk across dimensions:

**Financial Risk:**
- Financial stability
- Credit risk
- Dependency (SDI share of revenue)

**Operational Risk:**
- Capacity constraints
- Single points of failure
- Geographic concentration
- Technology obsolescence

**Compliance Risk:**
- Regulatory compliance
- Ethics compliance
- Certification status
- Litigation exposure

**Reputational Risk:**
- Public perception
- Labor practices
- Environmental record
- Association risk

**Security Risk:**
- Data handling practices
- Cybersecurity posture
- Physical security
- Insider threat

### 9.2 Risk Tiers

Vendors classified by composite risk:

**Tier 1 - Critical:**
High risk, high impact. Intensive monitoring, mitigation required.

**Tier 2 - Elevated:**
Moderate risk requiring active management and contingency planning.

**Tier 3 - Standard:**
Acceptable risk with normal monitoring and management.

**Tier 4 - Low:**
Minimal risk, routine management sufficient.

### 9.3 Risk Mitigation

Mitigation strategies by risk type:

**Financial Risk:**
- Financial monitoring
- Payment term adjustments
- Parent guarantees
- Escrow arrangements
- Alternative vendor qualification

**Operational Risk:**
- Dual sourcing
- Safety stock
- Capacity reservations
- Business continuity requirements
- Geographic diversification

**Compliance Risk:**
- Certification requirements
- Audit programs
- Training requirements
- Contract provisions
- Monitoring systems

**Security Risk:**
- Security requirements
- Assessment and testing
- Incident response provisions
- Data protection controls
- Insurance requirements

### 9.4 Concentration Risk

SDI manages vendor concentration risk through:
- Maximum spend thresholds with single vendors
- Category diversification requirements
- Geographic distribution targets
- Regular concentration analysis and reporting

## 11. Vendor Transition and Exit

### 11.1 Exit Planning

Exit planning required for:
- All strategic vendor relationships
- Critical service providers
- Single-source vendors
- High-risk vendors

Exit plans include:
- Transition triggers
- Alternative vendors
- Transition timeline
- Knowledge transfer requirements
- Data/asset return
- Communication plan

### 11.2 Transition Process

**Planned Transitions:**
1. Transition decision and communication
2. Alternative vendor selection
3. Parallel operation period
4. Knowledge transfer
5. Asset/data migration
6. Contract close-out
7. Relationship conclusion

**Unplanned Transitions (Vendor Failure):**
1. Crisis response activation
2. Business continuity measures
3. Emergency sourcing
4. Accelerated transition
5. Root cause analysis
6. Plan improvement

### 11.3 Contract Termination

Termination types:
- Convenience: Per contract notice provisions
- Cause: Material breach, compliance failure, performance failure
- Mutual: Agreed conclusion

Termination requirements:
- Legal review
- Notice per contract
- Transition planning
- Data/asset return
- Final payments/disputes
- Documentation
