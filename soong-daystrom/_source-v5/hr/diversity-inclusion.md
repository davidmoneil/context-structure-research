## Our Commitment

Soong-Daystrom Industries is committed to building a diverse, equitable, and inclusive workplace where every employee can thrive and contribute their best work. We believe that diverse perspectives drive innovation, and inclusion enables us to attract and retain the exceptional talent needed to achieve our mission.

### Vision Statement

*"To be recognized as an industry leader in creating an inclusive workplace where employees of all backgrounds feel valued, respected, and empowered to achieve their full potential while driving breakthrough innovation in AI and robotics."*

### Core Principles

**Diversity**: We value the unique backgrounds, perspectives, and experiences that each employee brings. We actively seek to increase representation across all dimensions of diversity.

**Equity**: We provide fair treatment, access, opportunity, and advancement for all employees. We identify and eliminate barriers that have prevented the full participation of all groups.

**Inclusion**: We create an environment where all employees feel welcomed, respected, supported, and valued. We ensure diverse voices are heard and integrated into decision-making.

**Belonging**: We foster a sense of community where employees feel they can bring their authentic selves to work without fear of judgment or exclusion.

## Strategic Priorities (2124-2126)

### Priority 1: Increase Representation in Technical Roles

**Goal**: Achieve 30% women and 22% URM in technical roles by 2126

**Key Initiatives**:
- Expand university partnerships with HBCUs, HSIs, and women's colleges
- Double investment in technical apprenticeship program
- Require diverse interview panels for all technical hires
- Partner with organizations like /dev/color, AnitaB.org, and Out in Tech
- Establish satellite engineering offices in diverse talent markets

### Priority 2: Accelerate Advancement of Underrepresented Groups

**Goal**: Achieve proportional representation in leadership (Director+) within 5 years

**Key Initiatives**:
- Sponsorship program pairing high-potential URG employees with executives
- Inclusive leadership development track
- Promotion readiness coaching program
- Transparent succession planning with diversity lens
- Regular analysis of promotion velocity by demographic

### Priority 3: Foster Inclusive Culture

**Goal**: Achieve 85% inclusion score on employee survey (currently 78%)

**Key Initiatives**:
- Mandatory inclusive leadership training for all managers
- Psychological safety workshops for all teams
- Allyship skill-building programs
- Inclusion nudges integrated into systems and processes
- Regular listening sessions with diverse employee groups

### Priority 4: Embed Equity in Systems and Processes

**Goal**: Eliminate statistically significant pay and opportunity gaps

**Key Initiatives**:
- Annual pay equity analysis and remediation
- Blind resume review pilot expansion
- Structured interview process to reduce bias
- AI-assisted job description review for inclusive language
- Equitable access to high-visibility assignments

## Accessibility and Accommodations

### Commitment to Accessibility

SDI is committed to ensuring all employees can fully participate regardless of disability status.

### Accommodation Process

1. **Request**: Employee submits accommodation request through HR portal
2. **Interactive Dialogue**: HR works with employee to understand needs
3. **Medical Documentation**: Obtained if needed (confidential)
4. **Solution Development**: Collaborative identification of accommodations
5. **Implementation**: Accommodation provided (typically within 14 days)
6. **Follow-up**: Check-in at 30 and 90 days

### Common Accommodations

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Physical | Ergonomic equipment, standing desks, accessible parking |
| Sensory | Screen readers, captioning, sign language interpreters |
| Cognitive | Quiet workspaces, written instructions, extended deadlines |
| Schedule | Flexible hours, remote work, modified schedules |
| Communication | Alternative formats, communication devices |

### Accessibility Standards

All SDI facilities and digital properties meet or exceed:
- ADA Accessibility Guidelines
- WCAG 2.2 AA standards (digital)
- Section 508 compliance
- Local accessibility regulations

## Reporting Concerns

### How to Report

Employees who experience or witness discrimination, harassment, or exclusion should report through:

| Channel | Contact |
|---------|---------|
| Manager | First-line reporting |
| HR Business Partner | Direct outreach |
| Ethics Hotline | 1-800-SDI-ETHIC (anonymous available) |
| DEI Team | dei@soong-daystrom.com |
| Online | ethics.soong-daystrom.com |

### Non-Retaliation

SDI strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports concerns in good faith. Retaliation is a terminable offense.

### Investigation Process

1. Report received and acknowledged (within 24 hours)
2. Initial assessment and assignment to investigator
3. Investigation conducted (typically 30 days)
4. Findings communicated to relevant parties
5. Corrective action implemented if warranted
6. Follow-up to prevent recurrence
