## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Policy Purpose

This Intellectual Property Policy establishes the framework for the creation, protection, management, and exploitation of intellectual property at Soong-Daystrom Industries. The Policy ensures that SDI's innovations in synthetic consciousness, robotics, and artificial intelligence are properly protected while fostering continued innovation.

### 1.2 Scope of Application

This Policy applies to:
- All employees of SDI and its subsidiaries worldwide
- Contractors, consultants, and temporary personnel
- Research partners and academic collaborators
- Joint venture participants
- Licensees of SDI technology
- Third parties interacting with SDI intellectual property

### 1.3 Strategic Importance

Intellectual property represents the core competitive advantage of Soong-Daystrom Industries. Our patent portfolio, trade secrets, and proprietary technologies are valued at over 2.3 trillion credits and form the foundation of our market leadership in synthetic consciousness systems.

## 3. Employee Inventions and Creations

### 3.1 Ownership of Employee Inventions

All inventions, discoveries, improvements, and innovations created by SDI employees during their employment belong to Soong-Daystrom Industries if they:

- Are made within the scope of employment
- Relate to SDI's current or anticipated business
- Are made using SDI resources, facilities, or information
- Result from work assigned by SDI

### 3.2 Invention Assignment Agreement

All employees must sign the SDI Invention Assignment Agreement (IAA) as a condition of employment. The IAA requires:
- Assignment of all qualifying inventions to SDI
- Prompt disclosure of all inventions
- Cooperation in patent prosecution
- Maintenance of confidentiality

### 3.3 Invention Disclosure Process

Employees must disclose potentially patentable inventions within 10 business days of conception:

1. Complete Invention Disclosure Form (IDF-100)
2. Submit to immediate supervisor and IP Department
3. Participate in invention review meeting
4. Provide additional information as requested
5. Cooperate with patent counsel in application preparation

### 3.4 Inventor Recognition Program

SDI recognizes and rewards inventors through:

| Achievement | Recognition |
|-------------|-------------|
| Invention Disclosure | 500 credit bonus |
| Patent Application Filed | 2,500 credits |
| Patent Granted | 5,000 credits |
| Strategic Patent | 10,000-50,000 credits |
| Licensing Revenue | Revenue sharing per policy |

### 3.5 Prior Inventions

Employees must disclose any prior inventions they wish to exclude from the IAA at time of hire. Such inventions must be:
- Documented on Prior Invention Schedule
- Not related to SDI business areas
- Not improved or further developed during employment

## 4. Third-Party Intellectual Property

### 4.1 Respect for Third-Party Rights

SDI respects the intellectual property rights of others and requires all personnel to:
- Never infringe patents, copyrights, or trademarks
- Properly license required third-party technology
- Conduct freedom-to-operate analysis before development
- Report potential infringement issues immediately

### 4.2 Third-Party Software

Use of third-party software must comply with:
- License terms and conditions
- Open source license obligations
- Export control regulations
- SDI software procurement policies

### 4.3 Open Source Software

Open source software use requires:
- Review and approval by Legal and Engineering
- Compliance with applicable license terms
- Documentation of all open source components
- Isolation from proprietary SDI code where required

**Prohibited Licenses**: Licenses that would require disclosure of SDI proprietary code (e.g., strong copyleft licenses) may not be used in SDI products without explicit approval.

### 4.4 Incoming Technology Review

All incoming technology must be reviewed for:
- Ownership and right to license
- Encumbrances and restrictions
- Third-party IP incorporated
- Export control classification
- Compatibility with SDI products

## 6. Trade Secret Protection

### 6.1 Classification System

| Classification | Description | Access |
|----------------|-------------|--------|
| SDI PUBLIC | Approved for public release | Unrestricted |
| SDI INTERNAL | General business information | All employees |
| SDI CONFIDENTIAL | Sensitive business information | Need-to-know |
| SDI SECRET | Critical competitive information | Restricted list |
| SDI ULTRA | Core consciousness algorithms | Named individuals only |

### 6.2 Protection Measures

All trade secrets must be protected by:

**Physical Security**
- Secured facilities with access controls
- Clean desk policy enforcement
- Secure destruction of documents
- Visitor escort requirements

**Digital Security**
- Encryption at rest and in transit
- Access logging and monitoring
- Data loss prevention systems
- Network segmentation

**Personnel Security**
- Background investigations
- Non-disclosure agreements
- Exit interviews and certification
- Ongoing training and awareness

### 6.3 Non-Disclosure Agreements

NDAs are required for:
- All employees and contractors
- Vendors with access to confidential information
- Partners in technology discussions
- Customers receiving confidential technical data
- Visitors to SDI facilities

### 6.4 Trade Secret Incidents

Suspected trade secret compromise must be reported immediately:
- Contact Security Operations Center
- Notify Legal Department
- Preserve all evidence
- Do not attempt independent investigation

## 7. Enforcement and Defense

SDI actively protects its intellectual property rights through:
