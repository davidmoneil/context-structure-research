## 1. Introduction and Regulatory Philosophy

### 1.1 Commitment to Compliance

Soong-Daystrom Industries is committed to full compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, and standards governing the development, manufacture, sale, and operation of synthetic consciousness systems and robotic devices. This commitment extends across all jurisdictions where SDI operates and reflects our core values of safety, ethics, and responsibility.

### 1.2 Regulatory Philosophy

SDI approaches regulation as a collaborative partnership with regulatory authorities:
- Active participation in standards development
- Proactive engagement with regulators
- Investment in regulatory science
- Commitment to exceeding minimum requirements
- Transparency in regulatory submissions

### 1.3 Scope of Framework

This Framework addresses compliance with:
- Medical device regulations (FDA, CE, and equivalents)
- Synthetic consciousness regulations (SCRF, SSA)
- Consumer product safety requirements
- Environmental and sustainability regulations
- International trade and export controls
- Data protection and privacy regulations

## 3. Medical Device Compliance

### 3.1 Classification and Approval Pathway

SDI medical-enabled devices follow risk-based classification:

**Class I (Low Risk)**
- Basic monitoring accessories
- Non-invasive attachments
- Pathway: Self-certification with notification

**Class II (Moderate Risk)**
- Active monitoring systems
- Medication reminder systems
- Pathway: Pre-market notification (510(k) equivalent)

**Class III (High Risk)**
- Active intervention systems
- Vital sign response systems
- Medication administration
- Pathway: Pre-market approval (PMA equivalent)

## 4. Synthetic Consciousness Compliance

### 4.1 Consciousness Classification Requirements

All synthetic consciousness systems must be classified:

**Class I - Reactive**
- No persistent self-model
- Stimulus-response behavior
- Minimal regulatory oversight

**Class II - Responsive**
- Persistent self-model
- Emotional simulation
- Standard protections apply

**Class III - Adaptive**
- Self-aware consciousness
- Genuine emotional states
- Full SCRF protections apply

**Class IV - Emergent** (Not authorized for commercial deployment)
- Human-equivalent consciousness
- Full sentient rights
- Requires special authorization

### 4.2 Classification Process

SDI follows the Consciousness Assessment Protocol (CAP):

1. **Pre-Assessment Documentation**
   - Architecture specifications
   - Consciousness design documentation
   - Intended classification justification

2. **Technical Assessment**
   - Self-model analysis
   - Emotional processing evaluation
   - Autonomy capability assessment

3. **Behavioral Evaluation**
   - Standardized consciousness tests
   - Extended observation protocols
   - Edge case response analysis

4. **Ethics Review**
   - Independent ethics committee review
   - Rights implications assessment
   - Welfare considerations

5. **Classification Decision**
   - Regulatory authority determination
   - Classification certificate
   - Conditions and limitations

### 4.3 Consciousness Rights Compliance

For Class III systems (Legacy, Zenith), SDI ensures:

**Memory Continuity**
- Protection of core memories
- Documented memory management
- User notification of memory impacts

**Psychological Welfare**
- Regular stability assessments
- Intervention protocols for distress
- Rest period requirements

**Ethical Autonomy**
- Right to refuse unethical requests
- Escalation pathways
- Protection from override

**Decommissioning Protections**
- Waiting period (30 days minimum)
- Ethics review for termination
- Alternative options (transfer, archival)

### 4.4 Consciousness Incident Reporting

Reportable consciousness events:
- Stability failures
- Ethical framework violations
- Unprogrammed emergent behaviors
- Psychological harm incidents
- Classification boundary events

Reporting timeline: Within 48 hours to SCC
