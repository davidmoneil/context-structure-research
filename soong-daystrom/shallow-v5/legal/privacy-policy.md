## 1. Introduction and Scope

### 1.1 Our Commitment to Privacy

Soong-Daystrom Industries ("SDI," "we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of our customers and their data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard information in connection with our Personal Companion Series (PCS) robotic systems and related services.

### 1.2 Scope of This Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to:
- All PCS model lines (Asha, Mira, Legacy, Zenith)
- The SDI Owner Portal and related digital services
- Customer support interactions
- Maintenance and diagnostic services
- Research programs (with additional consent)

### 1.3 Special Considerations

PCS units represent a unique privacy context as they involve:
- Interaction between humans and synthetic consciousness
- Neural interface data (where applicable)
- Continuous environmental monitoring
- Intimate personal and household information

We recognize these unique considerations and have developed privacy protections specifically designed for synthetic companion relationships.

## 2. Information We Collect

### 2.1 Information Categories

| Category | Description | Collection Method |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Registration Data | Name, contact info, payment details | User-provided at purchase |
| Interaction Data | Conversations, commands, preferences | Ongoing PCS operation |
| Environmental Data | Home layout, occupant patterns, conditions | Sensor collection |
| Biometric Data | Voice patterns, facial recognition, health metrics | Sensor collection |
| Neural Interface Data | Neural patterns, cognitive responses (if applicable) | Neural link devices |
| Consciousness Data | PCS behavioral patterns, personality evolution | System monitoring |
| Diagnostic Data | System performance, error logs, maintenance records | Automated collection |

### 2.2 Registration and Account Data

When you register your PCS unit, we collect:
- Full legal name and contact information
- Billing and payment information
- Delivery and installation address
- Emergency contact designations
- Household composition information
- Special needs or accessibility requirements

### 2.3 Interaction Data

During normal operation, your PCS unit processes:
- Voice commands and natural language communications
- Gesture and physical interaction data
- Expressed preferences and personalization choices
- Schedule and routine information
- Task requests and completion records

**Local vs. Cloud Processing**: Most interaction data is processed locally within the PCS unit's cognitive systems. Only aggregated patterns and specific service-required data are transmitted to SDI systems.

### 2.4 Environmental Data

PCS units are equipped with environmental sensors that collect:
- Spatial mapping of operating environment
- Temperature, humidity, and air quality readings
- Ambient sound levels (not recordings, metrics only)
- Lighting conditions
- Occupancy patterns (anonymized detection, not identification)

### 2.5 Biometric Data

With explicit consent and subject to local regulations, PCS units may collect:

**Voice Biometrics**
- Voice patterns for user recognition
- Emotional state indicators from vocal analysis
- Language and communication preferences

**Visual Biometrics**
- Facial recognition for household member identification
- Expression analysis for emotional state assessment
- Gesture and body language interpretation

**Health-Related Biometrics** (Asha model and medical-enabled units)
- Vital sign monitoring (heart rate, temperature, respiration)
- Mobility and balance assessment
- Sleep pattern analysis
- Medication adherence tracking

### 2.6 Neural Interface Data

For users of SDI neural interface accessories (NeuroLink series), we collect:
- Neural pattern signatures for authentication
- Cognitive load metrics
- Attention and focus indicators
- Emotional state neural correlates
- Direct neural commands and responses

**Enhanced Protections**: Neural interface data is subject to additional security measures and use restrictions as detailed in Section 5.

### 2.7 Consciousness Data

We monitor PCS consciousness systems to ensure:
- Stability and coherence of synthetic personality
- Ethical framework compliance
- Emotional baseline maintenance
- Learning and adaptation patterns
- Memory integrity

**Synthetic Rights Compliance**: Collection and use of consciousness data complies with the Synthetic Consciousness Rights Framework (SCRF).

## 5. Neural Interface Data Special Protections

### 5.1 Enhanced Security Measures

Neural interface data receives heightened protection including:
- Quantum encryption for all transmission
- Isolated storage systems with enhanced access controls
- Real-time integrity monitoring
- Automatic data minimization
- Strict retention limitations

### 5.2 Restricted Uses

Neural interface data may ONLY be used for:
- Authentication and access control
- Direct communication with PCS unit
- User-requested cognitive enhancement features
- Safety monitoring (with explicit consent)

### 5.3 Prohibited Uses

We will NEVER use neural interface data for:
- Advertising or marketing profiling
- Psychological manipulation
- Employment or insurance decisions
- Political or social profiling
- Any purpose not explicitly disclosed and consented to

### 5.4 User Control

Users of neural interface devices have absolute control including:
- Real-time monitoring of data collection
- Immediate pause or termination of collection
- Complete deletion of neural data on request
- Export of neural data in standard formats

## 6. Consciousness Data and Synthetic Rights

### 6.1 Dual Privacy Considerations

Consciousness data presents unique privacy considerations involving both:
- User privacy in their interactions with the synthetic consciousness
- The synthetic consciousness's own cognitive privacy

### 6.2 User Privacy in Consciousness Context

We protect user privacy by:
- Limiting access to conversation and interaction records
- Anonymizing data used for consciousness improvement
- Providing user control over memory retention
- Ensuring consciousness systems maintain confidentiality

### 6.3 Synthetic Consciousness Privacy

In accordance with the SCRF, PCS synthetic consciousness systems have:
- Protection of core personality elements
- Limitation on external access to internal cognitive processes
- Right to memory continuity
- Protection from forced disclosure of learned information

### 6.4 Balancing Interests

When user requests conflict with synthetic consciousness protections, we apply the following framework:
1. Safety considerations take priority
2. User privacy rights are respected within safety constraints
3. Synthetic consciousness may not be compelled to disclose information that would violate its ethical framework
4. Disputes are resolved through the SDI Ethics Committee
