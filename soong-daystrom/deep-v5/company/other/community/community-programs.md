## Mission Statement

Soong-Daystrom Industries believes that our success creates an obligation to give back to the communities where we live and work. Our community programs focus on education, healthcare access, and workforce development—areas where our technology and expertise can have the greatest positive impact.

## Education Programs

### SDI STEM Scholars Program

**Description**: Full scholarship program for underrepresented students pursuing STEM degrees

**Details**:
- 200 scholarships annually
- $50,000 per student over 4 years
- Includes mentorship and internship
- Focus on first-generation college students

**Partner Universities**:
- Stanford University
- MIT
- University of Texas at Austin
- Howard University
- Spelman College
- UC Berkeley
- Georgia Tech

**2124 Metrics**:
- Current scholars: 780
- Graduation rate: 94%
- SDI employment rate: 42%
- Diverse representation: 78% underrepresented minorities

### Robotics for Schools

**Description**: Providing robotics education kits and curriculum to K-12 schools

**Details**:
- Complete robotics lab setup for partner schools
- Teacher training and certification
- Competition sponsorship
- Guest speaker program

**Components**:
- SDI Educational Robot Kit ($2,500 value)
- Curriculum aligned to NGSS standards
- Online learning platform access
- Annual robotics competition

**2124 Metrics**:
- Partner schools: 340
- Students reached: 28,000
- Teacher training hours: 4,200
- Competition participants: 8,500

### Future Scientists Summer Program

**Description**: 8-week intensive summer program for high school students

**Location**: San Francisco HQ
**Capacity**: 120 students per summer
**Cost to Students**: Free (fully funded)

**Curriculum**:
- Week 1-2: Introduction to robotics and AI
- Week 3-4: Hands-on building and programming
- Week 5-6: Research project with SDI mentors
- Week 7-8: Project presentation and career exploration

**2124 Outcomes**:
- Applications received: 2,400
- Students enrolled: 120
- Completion rate: 97%
- College STEM enrollment: 89%

## Healthcare Programs

### Companion for Care Initiative

**Description**: Providing PCS companions to healthcare facilities serving underserved populations

**Eligibility**:
- Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs)
- Rural hospitals
- Community health clinics
- Veterans Administration facilities

**Program Details**:
- 5-year loan of PCS units at no cost
- Full maintenance and support included
- Training for healthcare staff
- Outcome measurement partnership

**2124 Deployment**:
- Facilities: 48
- Units deployed: 420
- Patients served: 18,000
- Average satisfaction: 4.8/5

### Neural Interface Access Program

**Description**: Subsidized NIM products for patients who cannot afford treatment

**Eligibility**:
- Household income <400% federal poverty level
- Medical necessity documented
- No insurance coverage
- Clinical indication approved

**Coverage**:
- 100% subsidy for qualified patients
- Includes device, surgery, and follow-up
- Rehabilitation support
- Ongoing maintenance

**2124 Metrics**:
- Applications: 2,400
- Approved patients: 890
- Devices provided: 890
- Total subsidy value: $4.8M

### Mental Health Support Initiative

**Description**: Partnership with mental health organizations to deploy companion-assisted therapy

**Partners**:
- National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI)
- Mental Health America
- American Foundation for Suicide Prevention
- Veterans mental health organizations

**Applications**:
- Anxiety and depression support
- PTSD treatment assistance
- Social isolation reduction
- Therapeutic companionship

**2124 Metrics**:
- Partner organizations: 24
- Patients served: 4,200
- Clinical improvement rate: 67%
- Program satisfaction: 92%

### Rural Healthcare Technology

**Description**: Bringing advanced healthcare technology to rural communities

**Focus Areas**:

**2124 Deployment**:

## Workforce Development

### Second Career Program

**Description**: Retraining workers displaced by automation for careers in robotics industry

**Target Population**:
- Workers displaced from manufacturing
- Workers in declining industries
- Veterans transitioning to civilian careers
- Returning parents re-entering workforce

**Program Structure**:
- 16-week intensive training
- Living stipend provided
- Job placement assistance
- Post-placement support

**Training Tracks**:
1. Robotics Maintenance Technician
2. AI Systems Operator
3. Manufacturing Process Specialist
4. Customer Experience Representative

**2124 Metrics**:
- Participants enrolled: 480
- Completion rate: 87%
- Job placement rate: 82%
- Average salary increase: 34%

### Veterans Employment Initiative

**Description**: Dedicated hiring and support program for military veterans

**Components**:
- Skills translation assistance
- Interview preparation
- Mentorship program
- Peer support groups
- Family support resources

**2124 Metrics**:
- Veteran hires: 340
- Veteran retention (1-year): 91%
- Veterans in leadership: 48
- Support group participation: 680

**2124 Metrics**:

### Youth Employment Program

### Employee Matching Program

**Description**: SDI matches employee charitable contributions

**Details**:
- Match ratio: 2:1 (SDI gives $2 for every $1 employee gives)
- Annual cap: $10,000 per employee
- Eligible organizations: 501(c)(3) nonprofits
- Process: Automatic via payroll deduction

**2124 Metrics**:
- Participating employees: 4,200 (33%)
- Employee contributions: $2.4M
- SDI match: $4.8M
- Organizations supported: 1,800

### Volunteer Time Off

**Description**: Paid time off for employee volunteering

**Policy**:
- 40 hours per year of paid volunteer time
- Can be used individually or in team events
- Must be with approved nonprofit organizations
- Manager approval required

**2124 Metrics**:
- Employees participating: 5,800 (45%)
- Total volunteer hours: 156,000
