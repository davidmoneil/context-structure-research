## 1. EHS Policy Statement

Soong-Daystrom Industries is committed to protecting our employees, contractors, visitors, communities, and the environment. We believe that all workplace injuries, illnesses, and environmental incidents are preventable. Safety is a core value, not a priority that can be traded against production or cost pressures.

### 1.1 Policy Commitments

We commit to:

- Comply with all applicable environmental, health, and safety laws and regulations
- Provide a safe and healthy workplace for all employees, contractors, and visitors
- Prevent pollution and minimize environmental impact of our operations
- Continuously improve our EHS performance through objective setting and measurement
- Engage employees at all levels in EHS programs and decision-making
- Communicate openly with stakeholders about our EHS performance
- Hold all levels of management accountable for EHS results

## 3. Safety Programs

### 3.1 Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment

**Job Hazard Analysis (JHA):**
All tasks are evaluated using the JHA process documented in SDI-EHS-101:

1. Select job for analysis (priority given to high-risk, new, or changed tasks)
2. Break job into steps
3. Identify hazards for each step
4. Determine risk level (severity x probability)
5. Develop controls (hierarchy of controls)
6. Document and communicate
7. Review periodically or after incidents

**Risk Matrix:**

| Probability | Catastrophic | Major | Moderate | Minor |
|-------------|-------------|-------|----------|-------|
| Frequent | Critical | Critical | High | Medium |
| Likely | Critical | High | High | Medium |
| Occasional | High | High | Medium | Low |
| Seldom | High | Medium | Medium | Low |
| Unlikely | Medium | Medium | Low | Low |

### 3.2 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

**PPE Assessment:**
Each work area has a documented PPE assessment specifying required equipment.

**Standard PPE by Area:**

| Area | Required PPE |
|------|--------------|
| Manufacturing Floor | Safety glasses, steel-toe shoes, hearing protection (where posted) |
| Cleanroom | Cleanroom suit, gloves, shoe covers, hair net |
| Laboratory | Lab coat, safety glasses, gloves (task-specific), closed-toe shoes |
| Chemical Storage | Face shield, chemical-resistant gloves, apron, safety glasses |
| Maintenance | Safety glasses, gloves, steel-toe shoes, hard hat (where required) |

**PPE Program Elements:**
- Hazard assessment documentation
- PPE selection criteria
- Employee training
- Proper use and care
- Inspection and replacement
- Medical evaluations (respirators)

### 3.3 Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)

**Scope:** All equipment with potential for unexpected energization or release of stored energy.

**Energy Types Controlled:**
- Electrical
- Mechanical (including potential energy)
- Hydraulic
- Pneumatic
- Thermal
- Chemical
- Gravitational
- Radiation

**LOTO Procedures:**
1. Notify affected employees
2. Shut down equipment using normal procedures
3. Isolate energy sources
4. Apply lockout devices and tags
5. Verify zero energy state
6. Perform authorized work
7. Remove locks/tags (authorized employee only)
8. Restore equipment and notify employees

**Complex LOTO:**
Equipment requiring multiple isolation points uses group lockout procedures with lockboxes.

### 3.4 Confined Space Entry

**Permit-Required Confined Spaces:**
- Storage tanks
- Pressure vessels
- Cleanroom air handlers
- Wastewater treatment tanks
- Underground utility vaults

**Entry Requirements:**
- Entry permit completion
- Atmospheric testing (O2, LEL, toxic)
- Ventilation as required
- Attendant stationed at entry
- Rescue plan and equipment available
- Communication maintained

### 3.5 Fall Protection

**Requirements:**
- Walking/working surfaces 6 feet or more above lower level
- Leading edges
- Holes in floors, roofs, or platforms
- Elevated platforms without standard guardrails

**Methods (in order of preference):**
1. Elimination (work from ground level)
2. Guardrail systems
3. Safety net systems
4. Personal fall arrest systems (PFAS)

**PFAS Inspection:**
- Before each use by user
- Annual inspection by competent person
- Removal from service after fall event

### 3.6 Electrical Safety

**Qualified Person Requirements:**
- Training in electrical hazards and controls
- Understanding of arc flash boundaries
- Proper PPE selection and use

**Arc Flash Program:**
- Studies completed for all facilities (2122-2123)
- Equipment labeled with arc flash boundaries
- PPE categories assigned
- Annual review for system changes

**Electrical Safe Work Practices:**
- Work de-energized whenever possible
- Energized work requires specific authorization
- Follow NFPA 70E requirements
- Use appropriate test equipment

### 3.7 Ergonomics

**Program Elements:**
- Workstation assessments
- Job rotation schedules
- Lifting equipment and aids
- Ergonomic furniture and tools
- Early symptom reporting

**High-Risk Tasks:**
- Repetitive assembly operations
- Manual material handling
- Microscope work (Singapore)
- Computer workstations

**Injury Prevention:**
- Stretch and flex programs
- Task rotation requirements
- Ergonomic equipment investment ($850K in 2123)
- Physical therapy on-site (Austin, Singapore)

## 4. Incident Reporting and Investigation

### 4.1 Incident Classification

| Type | Definition | Reporting Timeline |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| First Aid | Minor injury, first aid treatment only | End of shift |
| Recordable | Medical treatment, work restrictions, or lost time | Within 24 hours |
| Lost Time | Employee misses work due to injury | Within 8 hours |
| Serious | Hospitalization, amputation, loss of eye | Immediate (OSHA within 24 hours) |
| Fatality | Work-related death | Immediate (OSHA within 8 hours) |
| Near Miss | Event that could have caused injury | Within 24 hours |
| Environmental | Release, spill, or exceedance | Immediate |

### 4.2 Incident Response

**Immediate Actions:**
1. Ensure scene safety
2. Provide first aid/medical attention
3. Secure the area
4. Notify supervisor and EHS
5. Preserve evidence

**Reporting:**
- All incidents reported via SafetyFirst electronic system
- Near misses encouraged and tracked separately
- Anonymous reporting option available

### 4.3 Investigation Process

**Investigation Team:**
- EHS representative (lead)
- Supervisor of affected area
- Employee representative
- Subject matter experts as needed

**Investigation Methods:**
- Witness interviews
- Physical evidence collection
- Timeline reconstruction
- Root cause analysis (5 Why, Fishbone)
- Barrier analysis

**Documentation:**
- Incident Investigation Report (SDI-EHS-102-F)
- Corrective action plan
- Evidence photos and diagrams
- Witness statements

### 4.4 Corrective Actions

**Hierarchy of Controls:**
1. Elimination - Remove the hazard entirely
2. Substitution - Replace with less hazardous alternative
3. Engineering Controls - Isolate people from hazard
4. Administrative Controls - Change work procedures
5. PPE - Protect the worker (last resort)

**Tracking:**
- All corrective actions tracked in SafetyFirst
- Due dates assigned based on risk level
- Weekly follow-up until closure
- Effectiveness verification at 90 days
