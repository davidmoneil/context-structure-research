# Data Privacy Policy

**Policy Number**: POL-2024-002
**Effective Date**: January 1, 2118
**Last Revised**: June 15, 2124
**Owner**: Dr. Robert Kim (General Counsel)
**Approved By**: Board of Directors

## Purpose

This policy establishes the principles, standards, and procedures for collecting, processing, storing, and protecting personal data at Soong-Daystrom Industries, ensuring compliance with global privacy regulations and maintaining customer trust.

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All personal data processed by Soong-Daystrom
- All employees, contractors, and third parties with data access
- All products, services, and internal systems
- All global operations and facilities

## Definitions

**Personal Data**: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

**Sensitive Personal Data**: Data revealing racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, health data, biometric data, or genetic data.

**Neural Data**: Data derived from brain-computer interfaces (NIM products). Treated as sensitive personal data with additional protections.

**Processing**: Any operation performed on personal data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, or deletion.

**Data Subject**: The individual whose personal data is being processed.

## Core Principles

### 1. Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency
- Process data only with valid legal basis
- Be transparent about data practices
- Provide clear privacy notices

### 2. Purpose Limitation
- Collect data only for specified, explicit purposes
- Do not process data beyond original purposes
- Document all data processing purposes

### 3. Data Minimization
- Collect only data that is necessary
- Regularly review data holdings
- Delete unnecessary data promptly

### 4. Accuracy
- Ensure personal data is accurate
- Provide mechanisms for correction
- Update data regularly

### 5. Storage Limitation
- Retain data only as long as necessary
- Implement retention schedules
- Securely delete data after retention period

### 6. Security
- Implement appropriate technical measures
- Implement appropriate organizational measures
- Protect against unauthorized access

### 7. Accountability
- Document compliance efforts
- Maintain processing records
- Demonstrate compliance upon request

## Data Classification

### Tier 1: Public Data
**Definition**: Data intended for public disclosure
**Examples**: Press releases, public financial reports, marketing materials
**Protection**: Standard controls

### Tier 2: Internal Data
**Definition**: Data for internal business use
**Examples**: Employee directories, internal policies, meeting notes
**Protection**: Access controls, encryption in transit

### Tier 3: Confidential Data
**Definition**: Sensitive business data
**Examples**: Financial projections, strategic plans, customer lists
**Protection**: Encryption at rest and in transit, access logging, need-to-know

### Tier 4: Restricted Data
**Definition**: Highly sensitive personal or business data
**Examples**: Neural data, health data, security credentials, consciousness research
**Protection**: Maximum controls, special authorization, audit trails, isolation

## Neural Data Special Provisions

Given the unique sensitivity of neural data from NIM products:

### Collection
- Explicit informed consent required
- Purpose limitation strictly enforced
- Minimum necessary collection only

### Processing
- Processing only for consented purposes
- No secondary use without new consent
- Anonymization required for research use

### Storage
- Encrypted with customer-controlled keys
- Stored in jurisdiction of customer choice
- Retention limited to active use period + 90 days

### Sharing
- Never sold to third parties
- Sharing only with explicit consent
- No use for advertising or profiling

### Rights
- Real-time data access
- Immediate deletion on request
- Portable data export

## Data Subject Rights

### Right to Access
Data subjects may request a copy of their personal data.
**Response Time**: 30 days (45 for complex requests)
**Process**: Privacy portal or privacy@soong-daystrom.com

### Right to Rectification
Data subjects may request correction of inaccurate data.
**Response Time**: 14 days
**Process**: Self-service portal or support request

### Right to Erasure
Data subjects may request deletion of their data.
**Response Time**: 30 days
**Exceptions**: Legal retention requirements, ongoing service needs

### Right to Data Portability
Data subjects may receive their data in machine-readable format.
**Response Time**: 30 days
**Format**: JSON, CSV, or industry-standard format

### Right to Object
Data subjects may object to certain processing activities.
**Scope**: Marketing, profiling, automated decision-making
**Response Time**: Immediate for marketing; 30 days for others

### Right to Restrict Processing
Data subjects may request limitation of data processing.
**Scope**: During dispute resolution or verification
**Response Time**: 14 days

## Data Retention

### Standard Retention Periods

| Data Type | Retention Period | Basis |
|-----------|------------------|-------|
| Customer account data | Account life + 3 years | Contract |
| Transaction records | 7 years | Legal requirement |
| Support tickets | 3 years | Business need |
| Marketing data | Until consent withdrawn | Consent |
| Employee data | Employment + 7 years | Legal requirement |
| Neural data | Active use + 90 days | Consent/Contract |
| Research data | Study completion + 10 years | Regulatory |

### Deletion Process
1. Data identified for deletion
2. Verification of retention period completion
3. Backup purge scheduled (30 days)
4. Primary data deletion
5. Backup data deletion
6. Deletion certificate generated

## Security Requirements

### Technical Measures
- Encryption: AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit
- Access control: Role-based, least privilege
- Monitoring: Real-time security monitoring
- Backup: Encrypted, geographically distributed
- Incident detection: 24/7 SOC operations

### Organizational Measures
- Privacy training: Annual for all employees
- Access reviews: Quarterly
- Vendor assessments: Annual
- Penetration testing: Semi-annual
- Privacy impact assessments: For new processing

### Physical Measures
- Data center certification: SOC 2 Type II
- Access controls: Biometric, logged
- Environmental controls: Fire, flood, temperature
- Visitor management: Escorted, logged

## International Data Transfers

### Approved Mechanisms
1. Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
2. Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)
3. Adequacy decisions (where applicable)
4. Customer consent (limited cases)

### Transfer Impact Assessments
Required for transfers to non-adequate jurisdictions.
**Assessment includes**:
- Legal framework analysis
- Supplementary measures needed
- Risk evaluation
- Documentation

### Restricted Transfers
Neural data may only be transferred to:
- Jurisdictions with comparable neural data protections
- With explicit customer consent
- Using enhanced security measures

## Breach Response

### Classification

| Severity | Definition | Response Time |
|----------|------------|---------------|
| Critical | Mass exposure of Tier 4 data | 1 hour |
| High | Exposure of Tier 4 or mass Tier 3 | 4 hours |
| Medium | Limited Tier 3 exposure | 24 hours |
| Low | Tier 2 exposure or failed attempt | 72 hours |

### Response Process
1. Detection and classification
2. Containment
3. Investigation
4. Notification (if required)
5. Remediation
6. Post-incident review

### Notification Requirements
- Regulators: 72 hours (where required)
- Affected individuals: Without undue delay
- Internal stakeholders: Immediately
