## Purpose

This Charter sets forth the responsibilities, authority, and operating procedures of the Board of Directors of Soong-Daystrom Industries, Inc.

---

## Board Responsibilities

### Oversight

The Board is responsible for oversight of the Company's business and affairs, including:

1. **Strategic Direction**: Approving the Company's strategic plan and monitoring execution
2. **CEO Selection and Evaluation**: Selecting, evaluating, and compensating the CEO
3. **Succession Planning**: Ensuring appropriate succession plans for CEO and senior management
4. **Risk Oversight**: Overseeing the Company's risk management framework
5. **Financial Oversight**: Reviewing and approving financial plans and monitoring performance
6. **Governance**: Ensuring effective corporate governance practices
7. **Stakeholder Interests**: Considering the interests of shareholders and other stakeholders

### Specific Responsibilities

**Strategic**:
- Approve annual strategic plan
- Approve major transactions (>$500M or 10% of assets)
- Approve entry into new business lines
- Approve significant capital expenditures (>$200M)
- Review competitive position quarterly

**Financial**:
- Approve annual operating budget
- Review quarterly financial results
- Declare dividends
- Authorize stock repurchases
- Approve debt financing (>$1B)

**Human Capital**:
- Hire and evaluate CEO
- Approve CEO compensation
- Review senior management succession plans
- Oversee corporate culture and values
- Approve equity compensation plans

**Risk and Compliance**:
- Oversee enterprise risk management
- Review cybersecurity program
- Ensure regulatory compliance
- Review ethics and compliance programs
- Oversee internal controls

**Ethics and Consciousness**:
- Oversee artificial consciousness ethics framework
- Review consciousness welfare standards
- Approve consciousness research protocols
- Monitor regulatory developments
- Engage with stakeholders on consciousness issues

## Board Composition

### Size and Structure

- Board size: 7-11 directors
- Current size: 8 directors
- Independent directors: Minimum 7 (current: 7)
- Management director: CEO only

### Director Qualifications

**Required Qualifications**:
- Integrity and ethical character
- Sound business judgment
- Sufficient time for Board duties
- Absence of conflicts of interest
- Ability to represent stakeholder interests

**Desired Experience** (collective Board):
- Technology/innovation
- Healthcare/medical devices
- Ethics/philosophy
- Finance/accounting
- Legal/regulatory
- International business
- Public company governance
- Risk management

### Independence Standards

Directors are independent if they have no material relationship with the Company. Specifically, a director is not independent if:

1. Currently employed by Company (or was within past 3 years)
2. Received >$120,000 from Company (other than director compensation)
3. Family member serves as executive officer
4. Affiliated with Company's auditor
5. Employee of company with material business relationship (>$1M or 2% revenue)

### Term Limits and Retirement

- Term: 1 year (annual election)
- Term limit: None (but reviewed annually)
- Retirement age: 75 (may be waived)
- Overboarding limit: 4 public boards total

## Board Leadership

### Chair

The Board Chair:
- Sets Board agenda
- Chairs Board meetings
- Facilitates Board discussions
- Ensures adequate information flow
- Leads Board self-evaluation
- Represents Board to shareholders

Current Chair: Robert Morrison (Lead Independent Director)

### Lead Independent Director

When the Chair is not independent, a Lead Independent Director shall:
- Chair executive sessions
- Approve Board agendas
- Call special meetings
- Serve as liaison with CEO
- Be available to major shareholders

### CEO

The CEO is the only management director and:
- Attends all Board meetings
- Provides reports and information
- Implements Board decisions
- Manages day-to-day operations
- Does not vote on compensation matters

## Board Committees

### Audit Committee

**Purpose**: Oversee financial reporting, audit, and internal controls

**Composition**: 3+ independent directors, all financially literate, 1+ financial expert

**Key Responsibilities**:
- Appoint and oversee external auditor
- Review quarterly and annual financial statements
- Oversee internal audit function
- Review internal controls
- Oversee compliance programs
- Review cybersecurity program

**Meetings**: Minimum quarterly

### Compensation Committee

**Purpose**: Oversee executive compensation and human capital management

**Composition**: 3+ independent directors

**Key Responsibilities**:
- Approve CEO compensation
- Approve executive compensation philosophy
- Oversee incentive compensation plans
- Review succession planning
- Oversee human capital management
- Approve equity grants

**Meetings**: Minimum quarterly

### Nominating and Governance Committee

**Purpose**: Oversee Board composition and corporate governance

**Composition**: 3+ independent directors

**Key Responsibilities**:
- Identify and recommend director candidates
- Oversee Board evaluation process
- Review governance guidelines
- Recommend committee assignments
- Oversee director education
- Review shareholder proposals

**Meetings**: Minimum quarterly

### Ethics Committee

**Purpose**: Oversee consciousness ethics and responsible AI development

**Composition**: 3+ directors (may include non-directors)

**Key Responsibilities**:
- Review consciousness research ethics
- Approve major consciousness research programs
- Oversee welfare standards
- Engage with external stakeholders
- Monitor regulatory developments
- Advise on product ethics issues

**Meetings**: Minimum quarterly (more frequent as needed)

## Board Operations

### Meetings

**Regular Meetings**:
- Frequency: 6 per year minimum
- Location: Rotating among major facilities
- Duration: 1-2 days
- Materials: Distributed 5+ days in advance

**Executive Sessions**:
- Frequency: Every regular meeting
- Participants: Independent directors only
- Chair: Lead Independent Director

**Special Meetings**:
- Called by: Chair, CEO, or 2+ directors
- Notice: 24 hours minimum
- Purpose: Time-sensitive matters

### Attendance

- Target: 100% attendance
- Minimum: 75% required
- Participation: In-person preferred, virtual acceptable
- Failure to attend: Addressed by Governance Committee

### Materials and Information

**Board Materials**:
- Distributed 5+ business days before meeting
- Include: agenda, presentations, financials, materials
- Accessible via secure Board portal
- Confidential treatment required

**Ongoing Information**:
- Monthly financial reports
- Significant developments (as they occur)
- Industry news and competitive intelligence
- Regulatory developments

### Director Compensation

**Annual Retainer**: $350,000
- 60% stock / 40% cash

**Committee Retainer**:
- Audit Chair: +$40,000
- Compensation Chair: +$30,000
- Other Committee Chair: +$25,000
- Committee Member: +$15,000

**Lead Independent Director**: +$50,000

**Meeting Fees**: None (covered by retainer)

**Stock Ownership Guideline**: 5x annual retainer

## Board Evaluation

### Annual Evaluation

**Process**:
1. Anonymous survey of all directors
2. Individual director assessments
3. Committee self-evaluations
4. Third-party facilitation (every 3 years)
5. Report to full Board
6. Action plan development

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Board effectiveness
- Meeting quality
- Information flow
- Committee functioning
- Director contributions
- Board dynamics

### Individual Director Assessment

- Conducted annually
- Peer feedback component
- Self-assessment component
- Lead Independent Director review
- Results inform nomination decisions

---

## Shareholder Engagement

### Communication

**Investor Relations**: Professional IR function
**Annual Meeting**: In-person, virtual option
**Proxy Access**: 3% / 3 years standard
**Shareholder Proposals**: Per SEC rules

### Director Availability

- Directors available at annual meeting
- Lead Independent Director available for major shareholders
- Committee chairs available for specific topics
- Governance Committee oversees engagement
