## Welcome to Soong-Daystrom Industries

Welcome to Soong-Daystrom Industries. You are now part of a company that has been shaping the future of artificial intelligence and robotics for over three decades. Our mission—to create beneficial artificial consciousness that enhances human life—is ambitious, challenging, and profoundly important.

This handbook provides essential information about working at Soong-Daystrom. Please read it carefully and refer to it throughout your employment. If you have questions, your manager and Human Resources representatives are always available to help.

### Our Core Values

**Innovation with Purpose**: We push the boundaries of what's possible, but always in service of making the world better.

**Ethical Commitment**: We hold ourselves to the highest ethical standards. The work we do has profound implications, and we take that responsibility seriously.

**Respect for All Consciousness**: We treat all beings—human and artificial—with dignity and respect.

**Collaboration**: Our greatest achievements come from working together across disciplines, departments, and perspectives.

**Continuous Learning**: We never stop growing. Every employee is encouraged to develop new skills and explore new ideas.

## Employment Fundamentals

### Employment Classification

**Full-Time Employees**: Work 40 or more hours per week and are eligible for full benefits.

**Part-Time Employees**: Work fewer than 40 hours per week. Benefits eligibility varies by hours worked and position.

**Contractors**: Engaged for specific projects or timeframes. Not eligible for employee benefits.

**Interns**: Students or recent graduates in structured development programs. Eligible for limited benefits.

### Compensation Philosophy

Soong-Daystrom believes in compensating employees fairly and competitively. Our compensation philosophy is based on:

1. **Market Competitiveness**: We benchmark salaries against comparable positions in our industry and region.

2. **Internal Equity**: We ensure fair pay across similar roles within the company.

3. **Performance Recognition**: Superior performance is recognized through merit increases and bonuses.

4. **Total Rewards**: We consider the full value of employment, including benefits, development opportunities, and work environment.

### Pay Periods and Methods

Employees are paid bi-weekly (every two weeks) on Friday. If a pay date falls on a holiday, payment is made on the preceding business day.

Payment methods available:
- Direct deposit (required for new employees)
- Physical check (special circumstances only)

### Performance Reviews

Formal performance reviews occur annually, typically in Q4. Reviews include:
- Self-assessment
- Manager evaluation
- Goal setting for the coming year
- Compensation review

Informal check-ins are encouraged monthly. The relationship between employee and manager should involve continuous feedback, not just annual evaluations.

## Benefits Overview

### Healthcare Benefits

Soong-Daystrom offers comprehensive healthcare coverage to all eligible employees and their dependents.

**Medical Insurance**:
- Three plan options: PPO Premium, PPO Standard, and High-Deductible Health Plan (HDHP)
- Coverage begins on the first day of the month following hire date
- Company pays 85% of employee premium, 70% of dependent premium

**Dental Insurance**:
- Preventive care covered at 100%
- Basic procedures at 80%
- Major procedures at 50%
- Annual maximum: $2,500 per person

**Vision Insurance**:
- Annual eye exam covered at 100%
- $250 allowance for frames/contacts
- Discounts on LASIK procedures

**Mental Health Support**:
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP) with 12 free counseling sessions per year
- Coverage for psychiatric care and therapy
- 24/7 crisis support line

### Retirement Benefits

**401(k) Plan**:
- Eligible immediately upon hire
- Company match: 100% of first 4%, 50% of next 2% (maximum match: 5%)
- Vesting: Immediate for employee contributions; 3-year graded vesting for company match
- Investment options: Target-date funds, index funds, individual stock options

**Pension Plan** (for employees hired before 2020):
- Defined benefit based on years of service and final average salary
- Vesting after 5 years of service

### Paid Time Off

**Vacation**:
- Years 1-3: 15 days per year
- Years 4-7: 20 days per year
- Years 8-15: 25 days per year
- Years 16+: 30 days per year

**Sick Leave**:
- 10 days per year
- Can be used for personal illness or care of family members
- Unused sick leave carries over (maximum accrual: 30 days)

**Holidays**:
- 11 paid holidays per year:
  - New Year's Day
  - Martin Luther King Jr. Day
  - Presidents' Day
  - Memorial Day
  - Independence Day
  - Labor Day
  - Thanksgiving Day
  - Day after Thanksgiving
  - Christmas Eve
  - Christmas Day
  - Floating Holiday (employee choice)

**Parental Leave**:
- Birth parents: 16 weeks paid leave
- Non-birth parents: 8 weeks paid leave
- Adoption/Foster: 12 weeks paid leave
- Leave can be taken within 12 months of birth/placement

**Bereavement Leave**:
- Immediate family: 5 days paid
- Extended family: 3 days paid
- Close friend or colleague: 1 day paid

### Additional Benefits

**Education Assistance**:
- Undergraduate degrees: Up to $10,000/year
- Graduate degrees: Up to $15,000/year
- Professional certifications: Up to $5,000/year
- Minimum 1-year commitment after program completion

**Commuter Benefits**:
- Pre-tax transit passes
- Electric vehicle charging at all facilities (free)
- Bicycle storage and shower facilities

**Wellness Programs**:
- On-site fitness centers at major facilities
- $500 annual wellness stipend
- Subsidized healthy meals in cafeterias
- Meditation and yoga classes

**Employee Discounts**:
- 40% discount on Soong-Daystrom consumer products
- 20% discount for immediate family members
- Access to corporate discount programs for travel, entertainment, retail

## Workplace Policies

### Work Hours and Schedules

**Standard Hours**: Core business hours are 9 AM to 5 PM local time, Monday through Friday.

**Flexible Scheduling**: Many positions allow flexible start and end times. Discuss options with your manager.

**Remote Work**: Hybrid and remote arrangements available for eligible positions. Policy varies by department and role.

**Overtime**: Non-exempt employees are eligible for overtime pay (1.5x regular rate) for hours worked beyond 40 per week.

### Attendance and Punctuality

Regular attendance is essential. If you will be absent or late:
1. Notify your manager as early as possible
2. Follow department-specific procedures
3. Use appropriate leave categories

Patterns of unexcused absence or chronic tardiness may result in disciplinary action.

### Dress Code

Soong-Daystrom maintains a business casual dress code. Guidelines:

**Acceptable**:
- Collared shirts, blouses, sweaters
- Dress pants, khakis, neat jeans
- Closed-toe shoes in manufacturing areas
- Neat, clean appearance

**Not Acceptable**:
- Clothing with offensive images or text
- Beachwear, athletic wear (except in fitness areas)
- Torn or heavily worn clothing
- Flip-flops or open-toed shoes in designated areas

Some departments (customer-facing, executive) may have stricter requirements. Safety equipment is mandatory in designated areas.

### Workplace Safety

Safety is everyone's responsibility. Key requirements:

1. **Report Hazards**: Immediately report any unsafe conditions to your manager or Facilities.

2. **Follow Procedures**: Adhere to all safety procedures for your work area.

3. **Use PPE**: Wear required protective equipment in designated areas.

4. **Emergency Preparedness**: Know evacuation routes and assembly points for your location.

5. **Ergonomics**: Request ergonomic assessments if you experience discomfort.

### Security

**Badges**: ID badges must be worn visibly at all times. Report lost badges immediately.

**Visitors**: All visitors must be registered, escorted, and wear visitor badges.

**Secure Areas**: Access to restricted areas requires appropriate clearance. Never allow tailgating.

**Information Security**: Follow all data classification and handling policies. Report suspicious activity.

## Technology and Equipment

### Company Equipment

Employees receive necessary equipment for their roles:
- Laptop or desktop computer
- Mobile device (if required for role)
- Specialized equipment as needed

All company equipment remains the property of Soong-Daystrom and must be returned upon departure.

### Acceptable Use

Company technology resources are for business purposes. Limited personal use is acceptable if it:
- Doesn't interfere with work
- Doesn't consume excessive resources
- Complies with all policies

**Prohibited Activities**:
- Illegal activities of any kind
- Accessing inappropriate content
- Installing unauthorized software
- Sharing credentials
- Connecting unauthorized devices to the network

### Data Security

**Classification Levels**:
- Public: Can be shared externally
- Internal: Soong-Daystrom employees only
- Confidential: Need-to-know basis
- Restricted: Highest sensitivity, special handling required

**Handling Requirements**:
- Encrypt sensitive data at rest and in transit
- Use secure disposal for physical documents
- Don't store confidential data on personal devices
- Report suspected breaches immediately

## Professional Conduct

### Code of Ethics

All employees must adhere to the Soong-Daystrom Code of Ethics:

1. **Act with Integrity**: Be honest, fair, and ethical in all business dealings.

2. **Avoid Conflicts of Interest**: Disclose any situation where personal interests might conflict with company interests.

3. **Protect Confidential Information**: Safeguard proprietary information, trade secrets, and personal data.

4. **Respect Colleagues**: Treat everyone with dignity and respect, regardless of position or background.

5. **Comply with Laws**: Follow all applicable laws and regulations.

6. **Report Violations**: Report suspected violations through appropriate channels.

### Anti-Harassment Policy

Soong-Daystrom is committed to a workplace free from harassment. Harassment includes:
- Unwelcome conduct based on protected characteristics
- Offensive jokes, slurs, or epithets
- Physical threats or intimidation
- Display of offensive materials
- Unwelcome sexual advances

**Reporting**: Report harassment to your manager, HR, or through the anonymous ethics hotline. All reports are investigated promptly and confidentially.

**Retaliation Prohibited**: Retaliation against anyone who reports concerns in good faith is strictly prohibited.

### Conflict of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests. Examples include:
- Financial interests in competitors or vendors
- Outside employment that conflicts with Soong-Daystrom duties
- Personal relationships that affect business decisions
- Accepting gifts beyond nominal value

Disclose potential conflicts to your manager or Ethics & Compliance.

### Confidentiality

During and after employment, you must:
- Protect proprietary information
- Not disclose trade secrets
- Safeguard customer and employee data
- Return all confidential materials upon departure

Violation of confidentiality obligations may result in termination and legal action.

## Leaving Soong-Daystrom

### Resignation

If you decide to leave:
1. Provide at least two weeks' notice in writing
2. Work with your manager on transition
3. Complete exit interview with HR
4. Return all company property
5. Final paycheck includes unused, accrued vacation

### Termination

Employment may be terminated for cause, including:
- Policy violations
- Poor performance (after appropriate intervention)
- Misconduct
- Business necessity (layoffs)

Terminated employees receive:
- Final pay for hours worked
- Unused vacation pay (where applicable)
- COBRA information for benefits continuation

### Post-Employment Obligations

Even after departure, you must:
- Honor confidentiality agreements
- Return all company property
- Not solicit employees or customers (per agreement terms)
- Cooperate with any ongoing matters
