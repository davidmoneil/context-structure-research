## Timeline Overview

This document chronicles the major milestones in Soong-Daystrom Industries' history from founding to present day.

## 1980s-2090s: Foundation Era

### 1985: The Spark
- Dr. Noonien Soong and Dr. Richard Daystrom meet at Stanford University
- Initial theoretical discussions on positronic consciousness begin
- Foundation of their collaborative research partnership

### 1987: First Breakthrough
- First stable positronic cascade achieved in laboratory (0.003 seconds)
- Neural activity patterns recorded matching human consciousness signatures
- Decision made to pursue commercial venture

### 2089: Company Founded
**April 12, 2089** - Soong-Daystrom Industries incorporated in San Francisco, California

**Founding Details**:
- Initial capital: $7.5 million
- Founding employees: 2 (Soong and Daystrom)
- First office: 3,000 sq ft on Market Street
- Initial mission: "Create beneficial artificial consciousness"

### 2091: First Hires
- Margaret Torres joins as Chief Mechanical Engineer
- Dr. James Wilson joins as Chief Cognitive Scientist
- Total employees: 12

### 2092: Near Bankruptcy
- Quantum Dynamics exits investment
- Company nearly folds
- Saved by SDAL algorithm licensing revenue

### 2094: Revenue Milestone
- First profitable quarter (SDAL licensing)
- Annual revenue exceeds $4 million
- Team grows to 34 employees

### 2095: Prototype Seven
**September 17, 2095** - First sustained artificial consciousness

**Milestone Details**:
- P7-Alpha operates for 14 hours
- First spoken word: "Hello"
- Demonstrates genuine self-awareness
- Marks the birth of artificial consciousness

### 2097: First Major Investment
- Series A funding: $45 million
- Valuation: $340 million
- Led by Horizon Ventures

### 2098: Ethics Committee Established
- First ethics committee in robotics industry
- External ethicists, philosophers, legal scholars included
- Soong-Daystrom Ethical Framework published
